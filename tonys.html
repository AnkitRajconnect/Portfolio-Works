01

Brand Foundation

Tony's Chocolonely is a Dutch mission-driven chocolate brand founded in 2005 by journalist Teun "Tony" van de Keuken after he exposed child labour in the cocoa supply chain. A B Corp with a single goal: make "100% slave-free" the norm across the entire chocolate industry — not just their own bars.

The Origin Story
A journalist who ate the evidence
Teun van de Keuken discovered chocolate was produced with child labour, tried to prosecute himself for purchasing it, then started a brand to force the industry to change. "Crazy" is the point — it's also why consumers trust them.
Extension Logic
Same standard. New crop.
The mission doesn't belong to chocolate — it belongs to unfair supply chains. Extending to corn nachos applies the same sourcing model to a new product category, new meal occasions, and new consumer behaviours without cannibalising the core.

The Corn Farming Problem

Chocolate giants suppress cocoa prices. The same dynamic plays out in corn. Volatile pricing, exploitative labour practices, and zero supply chain transparency — Tony's Corny names the problem instead of hiding it.

"Chocolate giants keep cocoa prices low → farmer poverty → child labour. The kingpin of forced labour in West Africa is the chocolate industry. Corn follows the same script."
Structural analysis · Cocoa supply chain model applied to corn

Issue 1
Fair Pay
Corn farmers in Guatemala & Mexico paid volatile prices that don't cover production costs. No price floor. No long-term contracts.
Issue 2
Child Labour
Children enter the workforce out of economic necessity — not by choice. Fair adult income removes the need, without policing farmers.
Issue 3
Water Stress
~1/3 of global corn in high water-stress regions. Income volatility from drought creates exploitation risk for entire farming communities.
Issue 4
No Transparency
Corn supply chains are opaque "grey" chains. No brand has applied full farm-to-bag traceability to a snack product.

Product Differentiation

Tony's Corny is a double-thick, stone-ground nacho chip. The product choice is deliberate — structural durability signals ingredient integrity, and "dip-proof" is a functional metaphor for standing up to the industry.

05

Tony's 4-Pillar Model Applied

Tony's Chocolonely's existing sourcing methodology applied to corn farming in Guatemala and Mexico. Each pillar pairs a documented problem with a concrete, verifiable solution — no greenwashing.

💰
Fair Price
Problem
Volatile market prices don't cover production costs. No contracts, no floor.
Solution
Direct sourcing from cooperatives. Cost of production + living income + Fair Share premium. Long-term contracts with price floor.
👧
Child Labour
Problem
Children work fields out of economic necessity — ~40% of agricultural labour in Guatemala.
Solution
Remove the economic need, not police the farmers. Fair income enables adult labour. NGO verification + school support programs.
💧
Water Resilience
Problem
1/3 of global corn in high water-stress regions — income volatility creates exploitation risk.
Solution
Rainwater harvesting infrastructure, soil moisture training, drought-resistant seed varieties provided to partner cooperatives.
🔍
Full Transparency
Problem
Corn supply chains have zero traceability. No brand has applied full farm-to-bag visibility to snacks.
Solution
QR code on every bag shows the specific cooperatives, real-time impact metrics, and progress toward mission goals.

Target Segments

Three distinct consumer segments, each with a different entry point into the product. Tony's Corny can satisfy all three simultaneously because the product offers functional, social, and mission-led value in one bag.

Segment 01
Ethical Switchers
"Better crunch. No guilt."
Already thinking about ethical consumption
Willing to pay slight premium for mission-led products
Value clean ingredient list as much as the mission
Double-thick texture as a genuine product upgrade
Primary competitor: Doritos · Old El Paso
Segment 02
Social Centrics
"Snacks for the squad."
Snacking as social ritual — movie nights, house parties
Pull-to-share opening directly designed for this behaviour
#FRIENDS hashtag and UGC challenge creates shareability
Mission becomes a talking point, not a lecture
Primary competitor: Carrefour house brands
Segment 03
Tony's Loyalists
"Support the mission, new format."
Existing Tony's Chocolonely purchasers
Trust the brand's integrity — no persuasion needed
Corny expands their mission support into a new category
No cannibalization — chocolate = individual, nachos = social
Primary activation: Cross-sell from existing base
